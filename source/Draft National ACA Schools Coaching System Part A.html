Draft National ACA Schools Coaching System Part A

GENERAL DRAFT ACA SCHOOLS COACHING POLICY:

he ACA supports the development of a National Schools Coaching System. Our Goal should be that every Malletsports Club has access to the benefits of the National Schools Coaching System. 

An energetic Club with a lively Schools Program will attract the interest of Parents and Government. The Policy recognises that Schools Coaching is a specialist area of Coaching which requires training and certification.

The National Schools Coaching System is made up of two parts :
Programs
and
The Sustainable Coach System.

The Programs will be written by the National Director of Schools Coaching in consultation with the Advisory Committee for Schools Coaching.

We accept that the content of the Program will be implemented in different ways in different states because of Curriculum differences, and even by different Clubs and Coaches as they have different mixes and interests.  We will attempt to provide Programs which are rich enough to satisfy most needs.

The Sustainable Coach System :
In addition to the Programs, we hope to create a system of Schools Coaching which might be sustainable, and overcome the lack of personnel who are trained and certificated.  We anticipate that if Clubs choose to empower themselves to implement the system, that either the National Director of Schools Coaching, or a possible National Sports Development Officer, will assist.  This might also be via the State Directors of School Coaching, and their Sports Development Officers.
This Policy sees Sustainability being created by moving the potential stream of income from Schools Coaching to a Professional Coach in each club.  In time, their efforts may also be supplemented by VET students who are putting time in for their experience. 

In addition, there is the Active After-School Community Program, which is well funded and will also mean Clubs can employ young graduates or students or retirees to be the Schools Coach.
As in tennis, these Coaches could be expected to help with other club needs, such as management, welcoming or Coaching.

With the Collaboration of the National Director of Schools Coaching, a National Sports Development Officer, the State Directors of Schools Coaching and the State Sports Development Officers, and the National Advisory Committee for Schools Coaching, a Program and a Sustainable Coach System can be created for Clubs who choose to empower themselves to implement.

Clubs which choose to empower themselves to create a Sustainable Coaching system, will need the help of the Director of Coaching for how a Schools Program works, and its content, and also need the help of an SDO to help them get in touch with Schools, find and employ a young coach, and help them start their business.  That Coach will hopefully earn enough to also do some of the Club Coaching.

MARKETING AND DOCUMENTATION :
This Policy will have to be backed up by written material, video and coaching aides. We will also need a Marketing Campaign and Strategy to enable a Club or Coach employed by a Club to get a number of Schools in. We will need template advertisements for Coaches, contracts, and documents outlining ideal relationships between Club and Professional Coach.

END OF POLICY

OPPORTUNITIES FOR PROGRAMS AND THE SUSTAINABLE COACH SYSTEM

Our Programs have to respond to opportunities which have opened up.
The Government is keen to see students participating in sports on a regular basis, for health, recreation, personal development and as an adjunct to learning disciplines, and as an adjunct to the development of thinking and creative skills.  Governments are seeing education as the beginning of a whole of life learning pattern.  They are also seeing education starting from Pre-school and are removing the boundaries that have traditionally existed.

The National Curriculum for sport has not been set yet, but some states have moved forward.  Victoria has a Victorian Essential Learning Standards policy which is in keeping with contemporary educational thinking.  We have to present our sport as a response to such policies.

In addition, there are opportunities for Secondary Students to study subjects like Malletsports and get credit for their work.  We can even look to partnership with particular schools to create Academies of Malletsports.  These Students move onto TAFE and then University to undertake Sport and Recreation Courses.

Our Policy also realises that we now have problems from an older hopeful but naive approach.  This came from goodwill in the 1990’s from organisations such as the Australian Sports Commission.  It meant that many coaches and members of clubs were seen as able to coach schools.  However, Governments now insist on Certification for Working with Children, and training for Coaching Children is also needed.  Malletsports does not have the volunteer capacity to undertake a systematic Schools Program.

OPPORTUNITIES FOR MALLETSPORTS NOT YET GRASPED

You don't go to the pool to be told you can only swim : you might want to dive, or play water volleyball.  

You don't go to the snow to be told you can only walk up hill with skies on. You want to race down, or get a snowboard, or cross country, or race.

These are whole sports worlds.
You shouldn't be coming to Malletsports and be told you can only play croquet.
We must be a Sports for all : with games and activities that as diverse as people want : some target, some invasion, some etc.

This is very evident in the range of games and activities we need to provide for Schools Programs and the Active After-Schools Community Program.  If we want to be one of the sports with a separate Booklet in this program, we will have to think from a much larger view of our Malletsports World.

RATIONALE OF NATIONAL SCHOOLS COACHING SYSTEM

The Rationale for this National Schools Coaching System is :
There are not enough coaches to do this work.
This is not going to change.
We have not got far enough into the School Curriculum and been seen as an Answer to the needs of Schools and Teacher.

Sadly, the lack of coaches also applies to general coaching in the Clubs for existing members.

The Two ball shot has never been taught at the Schools level, and this is a huge marketing and educational mistake.  It is our greatest asset.

We have to understand Contemporary Educational Theory,  or the new Curriculum, national  and state.

We have to understand that School aged children do not need competitive or aggressive play till later in their development.

Learning needs to be based on experiential, fun, discovery, problem-solving.

OVERVIEW

By creating a Whole of Life Coaching Program, we can provide coaching to Schools and by allowing a trained coach to access the stream of income produced by a Schools Program, we can get Coaching of an acceptable standard for Schools, and this may also lead to coaching for Club members.

The main obstacles are that the current club members may not accept the idea of a professional or paid coach.  Instead, they will question the value of a Schools program, and demand all resources be put into Coaching existing members.  This will not happen.

I have in this report concentrated on outlining possible Whole of Life Learning Schools Programs and how a Club could recruit and appoint a Coach, and develop a relationship with them.  The outlined draft is comprised suggestions for marketing to schools, advertising for coaches, and the beginnings of programs.  These are presented with Children’s characters and as normal development programs. 

I believe we are not in a position to debate whether or not we need to adopt the Sustainable Coach system I am describing.  We are already many years behind.

And, there is much work to be done.  I have arranged for us to actually be a constituent sport in the Active After-Schools Community Program, and we need to complete the Programs.  We also have to accept diversity in the execution of the programs, and in the sports a particular club or coach might want to coach. The real driving force for the progress of the sport is enthusiasm. To this end, I have recommended an Advisory Committee to help create what we need.

I would recommend that we look at the work of the following sports :
Tennis : This sport has a fully developed system, where Club Coaches are seen as the backbone of a Clubs, and they are helped in the development of small businesses, on the understanding that they are there to welcome new members, and coach more skilled ones.
Football : Both Australian Rules and Rugby even have Academies attached to Secondary Colleges, where student undertake VET and in that, they visit and coach Primary School Children.

We have a great sport in Malletsports, and need to communicate that better.

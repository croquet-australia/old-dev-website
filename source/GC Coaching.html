National Coaching Director

Greg Bury

Greg started playing croquet in 1981 at the age of 11. He is currently a proud member of the Headland Buderim Croquet Club, Sunshine Coast, Queensland. He has represented Australia in MacRobertson Shield, Trans Tasman and World Championship teams and has represented Queensland 28 times in Association and Golf Croquet. He is a previous Australian Singles and Doubles Champion. Greg recently qualified as a Level 3 Coach.

He’s very passionate about the development of players and Coaches with a view to improving the standard of play in Queensland and Australia.

State Coaching Director New Soth Wales

Barb Piggott

Barb is a member of the Urunga Croquet Club, situated approximately 25 km south of Coffs Harbour on the NSW mid north coast. She has been playing croquet since 2005 and enjoys both Golf Croquet and Association. She was selected to represent NSW at the Golf Croquet Interstate Shield in Brisbane in 2011 and in Melbourne in 2012. In August 2010 she qualified as a Level 1 Association Coach and completed the Level 2 Association Coaches training in July 2012. In 2011 she was appointed NSW State Director of Coaching and has conducted numerous Coaching Clinics throughout NSW since her appointment.

State Coaching Director Queensland

David Reid

David commenced playing at Port Macquarie in 1994. Having retired he returned to croquet at Headland Buderim in 2003 and advanced to a handicap of 8 before taking up coaching, the late Nell Hass guiding him to Level 1. David became club coach, quickly followed by that of Regional Coordinator. He is currently studying for qualification as a Level 2 AC Coach.

David considers himself to be an administrator and believes his range of policies and procedures will provide a firm base for future State coaching. His current coaching is directed towards the beginner and intermediate levels of players and Coaches, because this is where great players and good Coaches find their feet.

The Golf Croquet Coach Training Programs

(Please scroll down on page)

Golf Croquet Coach training programs will be introduced throughout ACA. All programs above Orientation (O) will soon be accredited with the National Coach Accreditation Scheme(NCAS). Meanwhile ACA and the States will provide certification for Coaches upon qualification.

Accreditation will be current for four years; to retain accreditation Coaches will be required to conduct a minimum number of hours coaching, to maintain adequate records of active coaching activities and to attend specified update workshops. Specific requirements will be advised early to mid 2013.

Golf Croquet Coach Level O (Orientation) Program is currently being trialled in Queensland. Level O Coaches will achieve competency in teaching skills and tactics contained in the Golf Croquet Level O Golf Coach Manual. To be introduced 2013.

This Program is designed to provide coaches with the ability to teach new players.

Golf Croquet Coach Level 1 Program Level 1 Coaches will achieve competency in teaching the skills and tactics contained in both the Level O and Level 1 Golf Coach Manuals. This program is designed to train coaches to teach intermediate and advanced players. To be introduced 2013.

Prerequisite: candidates must complete the NCAS Beginning Coaching Principles Course. www.ausport.gov.au/participating/coachofficial/generalprinciples/beginning

Golf Croquet Coach Level 2 Program

This program is under development. It will train Coaches of advanced and representative players.

Prerequisite: candidates must complete the NCAS Intermediate Coaching Principles Course. www.ausport.gov.au/participating/coachofficial/generalprinciples/intermediate

To be trialled in 2013.

Your role as a coach

Coaching is an important role and must be taken seriously. You must be committed, reliable, understanding and patient. You are a role model and need to be professional at all times.

You must have an agenda that is well planned and has clear objectives. Your students needs to feel welcomed, important and they must have your full attention at all times. You must remain calm and be in control.

You don’t have to be a good player to be a good Coach. If you are organised confident and in control of your sessions, you will earn the respect of your students and they will respond positively. You must remain positive at all times and establish an environment where students are comfortable to ask questions and take risks.

As a coach, your students are your responsibility. There is no greater satisfaction than watching students develop under your direction and guidance. This is what makes coaching so rewarding.
